<div class="alert alert-warning">
<p class="alert-title">Go EARLY — July is hazy season</p>
<p>Summer is the haziest, least reliable time to see Mt. Fuji, and the peak usually clouds over as the day heats up. Mornings give the best odds, so this day is built around an early start. We've also planned it so it's a great day <strong>even if Fuji hides</strong> — lakes, ropeway, coasters, and onsen don't need a clear summit.</p>
</div>

## Getting There

<div class="activity-block">
<div class="activity-time">🌅 Early Start — Tokyo → Kawaguchiko (~2 hours)</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Option A: Direct Limited Express Train</h3>
<p>The Fuji Excursion limited express runs direct from Shinjuku to Kawaguchiko Station — the most comfortable option.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Duration:</strong> ~2 hours, ~¥4,200 one way</li>
<li><strong>Seat reservations are MANDATORY</strong> — book ahead, only a few run per day</li>
<li>Take the earliest departure you can manage for the best Fuji odds</li>
</ul>
<div class="activity-meta">
<span>🚆 Shinjuku → Kawaguchiko</span>
<span>⏱️ ~2 hr</span>
</div>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Option B: Highway Bus from Shinjuku</h3>
<p>Fujikyu and Keio run direct buses (~2 per hour) from the Shinjuku Bus Terminal (Busta Shinjuku) to Kawaguchiko Station.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Duration:</strong> just under 2 hours, ¥2,200 (¥2,000 if booked online)</li>
<li>Cheapest option; reserve online in summer as seats sell out</li>
<li>Japan Rail Pass is not valid on these buses</li>
</ul>
<div class="activity-meta">
<span>🚌 Busta Shinjuku → Kawaguchiko</span>
<span>⏱️ ~2 hr</span>
</div>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Option C: Train Fallback (no direct express)</h3>
<p>If the direct express is sold out: JR Chuo Line Shinjuku → Otsuki (~70 min limited express / ~100 min local), then transfer to the Fujikyu Railway Line to Kawaguchiko (~55 min, ¥1,170).</p>
</div>

</div>

## Morning — THE VIEWS

<div class="activity-block">
<div class="activity-time">☀️ Morning — Best Fuji Odds</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Chureito Pagoda</h3>
<p>The classic postcard shot: a five-story pagoda with Mt. Fuji rising behind it. Reached from Shimoyoshida Station (one stop before Kawaguchiko on the Fujikyu line) — about 400 steps up to the viewpoint.</p>
<p><strong>Why first:</strong> Morning light is best and the mountain is most likely to be clear early. If you're coming by train, you can stop here before continuing to Kawaguchiko.</p>
<div class="activity-meta">
<a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Chureito+Pagoda">📍 Get Directions</a>
<span>⏱️ ~1 hour</span>
</div>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Lake Kawaguchi North Shore / Oishi Park</h3>
<p>The most accessible of the Fuji Five Lakes and one of the best close-up views of the mountain. On a calm morning you may catch Fuji reflected in the water ("Sakasa Fuji").</p>
<p><strong>Summer bonus:</strong> The north shore around Oishi Park often has lavender and seasonal flowers in late July, framed against the lake and mountain. Worth confirming the flower festival dates closer to the trip.</p>
<div class="activity-meta">
<a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Oishi+Park+Lake+Kawaguchi">📍 Oishi Park</a>
<span>⏱️ 1-2 hours</span>
</div>
</div>

</div>

## Midday

<div class="activity-block">
<div class="activity-time">🚠 Midday — Lakeside</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Kachi-Kachi Ropeway</h3>
<p>A short cable car up Mt. Tenjo above Lake Kawaguchi to a viewing deck looking straight at Fuji and down over the lake. Quick, easy, and a great vantage point.</p>
<div class="activity-meta">
<a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Kachi+Kachi+Ropeway+Kawaguchiko">📍 Get Directions</a>
<span>⏱️ ~45 min</span>
</div>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Lunch by the Lake</h3>
<p>Try Yoshida udon (a thick, chewy local noodle specialty) or grab a lakeside cafe with a Fuji view. Houtou (flat noodles in miso hot pot) is the other regional classic.</p>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Getting Around: Omni Sightseeing Bus</h3>
<p>The Omni retro buses are the easiest way to loop between the sights from Kawaguchiko Station:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Red Line</strong> (Kawaguchiko) — every ~15 min, covers the east/north lakeshore and Oishi Park</li>
<li><strong>Green Line</strong> (Saiko) — every ~30 min</li>
<li><strong>Blue Line</strong> (Shojiko/Motosuko) — every 1-2 hours</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Tip:</strong> A 2-day Omni bus pass pays off fast if you're hopping between viewpoints.</p>
</div>

</div>

## Afternoon — Pick Your Path

<div class="activity-block">
<div class="activity-time">🎢 Afternoon — Choose Your Own / Weather Backup</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Option 1: Fuji-Q Highland (Teens' Pick)</h3>
<p>One of Japan's best thrill-coaster parks, right by Kawaguchiko with Fuji as the backdrop. Record-setting roller coasters and a great rainy-/hazy-day fallback when the summit won't cooperate.</p>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> Pay-per-ride or a day pass. Big coasters can have long waits — check the app for times.</p>
<div class="activity-meta">
<a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Fuji-Q+Highland">📍 Get Directions</a>
<span>⏱️ Half day</span>
</div>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Option 2: Onsen with a Fuji View</h3>
<p>Several lakeside hot springs look out toward the mountain — a relaxed way to end the day. A calm, scenic alternative to the coasters if the group wants to slow down.</p>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Option 3: More Lake Time</h3>
<p>Lake cruise, kayaking, the Kawaguchiko Music Forest, or just more lakeshore wandering and photos. No wrong answer.</p>
</div>

</div>

## Weather Backup Strategy

<div class="alert alert-info">
<p class="alert-title">If Fuji is fully hidden</p>
<p>It happens often in summer — don't sweat it. The lakes, Kachi-Kachi ropeway, Fuji-Q coasters, onsen, and local food make this a great day regardless. Check a Fuji webcam (e.g. search "Mt Fuji Kawaguchiko live camera") the night before and that morning to set expectations and decide how hard to chase the early train.</p>
</div>

## Reservations & Booking

<div class="alert alert-warning">
<p class="alert-title">Book Limited Express Seats Ahead</p>
<p>If taking the direct Fuji Excursion express, <strong>seat reservations are mandatory</strong> and only a few trains run per day — book as soon as you lock the date. The highway bus also sells out in summer; reserve online for the cheaper fare. No tickets needed for the viewpoints themselves.</p>
</div>

## What to Bring

- [ ] Reserved train/bus tickets (or booked online)
- [ ] Layers — it's cooler at the lakes than in Tokyo
- [ ] Sunscreen + hat (lots of open lakeshore)
- [ ] Water bottle
- [ ] Cash (smaller spots and buses)
- [ ] Camera / phone charged for the Fuji shots
- [ ] Comfortable shoes (Chureito Pagoda steps)

## Why This Day Works

- Closest base to Fuji = the best chance of actually seeing the mountain
- Early start beats the summer haze and afternoon cloud build-up
- Iconic views (Chureito Pagoda + the lake) plus a teen-friendly coaster backup
- Flexible afternoon adapts to weather and energy levels
- Relaxed evening return sets up an easy departure day tomorrow
