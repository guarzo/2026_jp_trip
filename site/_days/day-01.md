## Afternoon

<div class="activity-block">
<div class="activity-time">✈️ Arrive 2:15 PM (DL295 from Atlanta)</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Airport → Hotel</h3>
<p>Clear customs, pick up eSIMs if not already activated, get IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) for trains, and head to the hotel.</p>
<p><strong>Get Yen:</strong> Exchange dollars to yen at the airport — highly recommended! Many temples, shrines, and smaller shops are cash-only. Airport ATMs (7-Eleven) also work great with foreign cards.</p>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Hotel Check-in</h3>
<p>Get settled, drop bags, freshen up after the long flight.</p>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Konbini Snack Run</h3>
<p>Welcome to Japan! Hit up 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, or Lawson for your first taste of Japanese convenience store magic.</p>
<p><strong>Must-try:</strong> Onigiri, egg sandwich, melon pan, Japanese iced coffee</p>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>🐷 Micro Pig Cafe (Asakusa)</h3>
<p>Short, low-key animal cafe experience right in Asakusa — a fun, AC-cooled jet-lag-friendly stop on the way to Sensoji. Book a late-afternoon timed slot via Klook.</p>
<p><strong>Tip:</strong> Pick a slot ~5 PM so you can roll straight into Sensoji + dinner. Plan for ~30 min inside.</p>

## Evening

<div class="activity-block">
<div class="activity-time">🌙 Evening — Sensoji + Sumo Show</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Sensoji Temple</h3>
<p>Tokyo's oldest temple is stunning in the evening — fewer crowds and beautifully lit up. Walk through the Thunder Gate (Kaminarimon), browse Nakamise shopping street, and take in the temple grounds.</p>
<p><strong>Why evening?</strong> Less crowded, cooler temperatures, and the lanterns are gorgeous after dark. The shops on Nakamise close around 5-6 PM, but the temple grounds are open and atmospheric.</p>
<div class="activity-meta">
<a href="https://maps.google.com/?q=Sensoji+Temple+Asakusa">📍 Get Directions</a>
<span>⏱️ 45-60 min</span>
</div>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>🥋 Asakusa Sumo Club — Show + Chankonabe Dinner</h3>
<p>Live sumo demonstration with retired wrestlers, audience interaction, and the traditional chankonabe hot pot dinner sumo wrestlers eat to bulk up. Perfect Day-1 anchor: in Asakusa already, structured timing, and dinner is handled.</p>
<p><strong>Why this works for Day 1:</strong> No decisions to make jet-lagged, AC venue, and an actually memorable "we're in Japan" first night.</p>
<div class="activity-meta">
<a href="https://www.klook.com/en-US/activity/109994-asakusa-sumo-club-sumo-show-admission-chankonabe-tokyo/">🎟️ Klook listing</a>
<span>⏱️ ~1.5–2 hr (includes meal)</span>
</div>
</div>

<div class="activity-card">
<h3>Fallback: Dinner Near Sensoji</h3>
<p><strong>If jet lag wins or the sumo show doesn't book:</strong> The Asakusa area has excellent traditional restaurants (sukiyaki, tempura, sushi) — or no shame in grabbing konbini food and calling it early. You have 12 more days for sit-down meals!</p>
</div>

## Tips for Day 1

- **Don't fight jet lag too hard** — Stay up until a reasonable local bedtime (8-9 PM is fine)
- **Hydrate** — Long flights are dehydrating
- **eSIM** — Should be activated before landing (set up at home before departure)
- **IC Card** — Get a Suica or Pasmo card at the airport for trains
